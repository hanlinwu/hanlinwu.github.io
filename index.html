Hanlin Wu

Lecturer, School of Information Science and Technology, Beijing Foreign Studies University

Hanlin Wu (吴瀚霖) received his B.S. in Statistics (2015) and Ph.D. in Computer Application Technology (2022) from Beijing Normal University (BNU). He is currently a Lecturer at the School of Information Science and Technology, Beijing Foreign Studies University (BFSU).

He has an academic background in mathematics, probability theory, and computer science. His research interests include:

  • Generative Image Restoration: Score-based and diffusion-style modeling for image restoration, structural prior learning, and data-efficient adaptation.
  • Multimodal Vision-Language Models: Vision-language representation learning for cross-modal retrieval, semantic alignment, and large-scale image-text understanding.
  • Remote Sensing Image Super-Resolution: Flexible remote sensing image enhancement across arbitrary zoom factors, with a focus on high-frequency detail recovery and efficient deployment.
